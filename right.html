About Me

Keith-Allen is a talented and dynamic performer with a passion for various types of dance, including Contemporary, Jazz, Ballet, Modern, Vogue, Tap, Hip-Hop, and more. He is especially passionate about choreography, with five years of experience captivating audiences through the fluidity, dynamics, and emotional depth of both his dancing and choreographic work.



Keith is currently training at Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School and is in his second year as a Young Professional at MOVE|NYC|. He has also trained at prestigious institutions such as Alvin Ailey, Ballet Hispánico, and most recently The Juilliard School. Throughout his journey, Keith has studied under renowned teachers, including:
  • Shay Bland
  • Greg Lau
  • Genessy Castillo
  • Milton Myers
  • Risa Steinberg
  • Terese Capucilli
  • Adrienne Hurd,
  • Rodney Hamilton,
  • Branndi Lewis,
  • Fredrick Earl Mosley
    • ...and many more, all of whom have helped refine his craft and expand his artistic range.


Keith’s performance credits include appearances at the St. George Theatre, Connelly Theater, and New York City Center for Dance Against Cancer, where he earned acclaim for his versatility, emotional depth, and stage presence. Additionally, he has a background in musical theater, having performed in Legally Blonde the Musical and Chicago the Musical with Kidztheater.

Keith's dedication to his art has earned him numerous accolades, including the Nuestro Future Award from Ballet Hispánico, an Artistic Advancement Scholarship from MOVE|NYC|, and recognition as a YoungArts Winner in Modern/Contemporary Dance for his original choreography. Outside of performing, Keith enjoys strength training, attending performances, and continuing to explore new ideas in choreography. His diverse background and ongoing commitment to growth enable him to inspire and connect with audiences on a profound level.
